About

I am a researcher and educator with interests spanning applied mathematics, control, numerical analysis, and process systems for manufacturing. At Bowling Green State University, I focus on mathematical frameworks that raise efficiency and product performance in glass production by integrating PDE-based modeling, control-oriented formulations, and computation.

  • Process modeling & control for manufacturing
  • Numerical methods & scientific computing
  • Data analysis with Python/R/MATLAB
  • Simulation (ANSYS) and molecular tools (LAMMPS/VMD)

Research Focus

Glass Manufacturing: Modeling, Control, & Optimization

Development of mathematical frameworks to improve production efficiency and product performance, combining applied mathematics, materials science, and process engineering. Emphasis on deterministic models with control-theoretic insights and numerical solvers.

  • PDE/ODE models & discretization for thermal/flow behavior
  • Control strategies & observer design for process variables
  • Computational experiments & sensitivity analysis

Scientific Computing & Multi‑Scale Methods

Numerical and computational techniques across scales—continuum models for processes and particle-level simulation baselines using LAMMPS/VMD for materials-related inquiries.

  • Finite difference/finite element baselines
  • Verification/validation and performance profiling
  • Reproducible workflows in Python/MATLAB/R
